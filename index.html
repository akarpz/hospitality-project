Community Service Form

The hospitality industry is a very people-oriented profession. As such it has been traditionally involved with many aspects of community service and support. It is our intent to expose all of our students to this vitally important role of the industry through the academic requirement of one hundred hours experience.

Recognizing that there are many venues of service to fulfill these hours, it becomes necessary to generally define community service. By definition, community service shall include activities for which you volunteer, and which are intended to provide aid and support to those in need and/or for the improvement of the community as a whole. Those activities that specifically benefit you or your organization financially shall not be accepted.

Examples of community service may include activity involvement with the following types of non-profit agencies:
  • animal shelters
  • community dining rooms
  • crisis centers
  • disaster assistance organizations
  • disease related organizations
  • food banks
  • group homes
  • homeless shelters
  • medical support and assistance groups(hospital)
  • municipal community organizations
  • religious aid groups
  • senior centers and youth groups.
Each community service activity requires a Documentation of Community Service form.

Acceptable non-profit agency supervisor signatures from community service events (including Sorority and Fraternity events) should not be students or relatives.

Certain community service events have a limit on the number of community service hours that will be accepted:
  • UDance-maximum 10 hours of community service per year
  • Food drives-maximum 4 hours[20 cans] per event {5 cans = 1 hour}
When you sign up to volunteer for an event, the organization is counting on your participation. Therefore, if you sign up to volunteer for an event and do not participate, five hours of community service will be deducted from your total hours.
